stage_2_migration
  Binary flag indicating whether the loan, currently classified as IFRS 9
  Stage 1 (performing, 12-month ECL), was reclassified to Stage 2 (significant
  increase in credit risk, lifetime ECL) at the next quarterly assessment date.
  A Stage 2 reclassification requires the bank to hold substantially higher
  loan loss provisions, directly impacting reported profitability and capital.
  Values : 1 (migrated to Stage 2), 0 (remained in Stage 1)
  Overall migration rate : ~22.0 %

── Loan Identity ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1.  loan_id
    Unique identifier for each loan in the portfolio.
    Format: LN-XXXXXXXX.

2.  assessment_date
    The quarter-start date at which this snapshot was taken.
    Format: YYYY-QN (e.g., 2023-Q3). Spans 2021–2024.

── Loan Classification ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────

3.  loan_type
    Product type of the loan.
    Values: Residential Mortgage | Buy-to-Let Mortgage | Personal Loan |
            Auto Finance | SME Term Loan |
            Corporate Revolving Credit Facility | Credit Card
    Unsecured products (Credit Card, Personal Loan) carry higher migration
    rates due to higher inherent PD and lack of collateral protection.

4.  origination_channel
    The channel through which the loan was originated.
    Values: Branch | Mortgage Broker | Online / Digital | Direct Sales |
            Relationship Manager

5.  bank_region
    Geographic region of the UK in which the borrower is located.
    Values: London | South East | Midlands | North West | North East |
            Scotland | Wales | South West

── Loan Terms ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

6.  loan_amount_gbp
    Original principal amount of the loan in GBP at inception.
    Range: £1,000–£5,000,000. Correlated with product type (mortgages highest,
    credit cards lowest).

7.  outstanding_balance_gbp
    Current outstanding balance on the loan at the assessment date.
    Range: £300–£4,900,000. Always ≤ loan_amount_gbp.

8.  original_loan_term_months
    Total contractual term of the loan in months at origination.
    Range: 12–300 months.

9.  remaining_term_months
    Number of months remaining until the loan's scheduled maturity date.
    Range: 1 month upward. Loans near maturity (< 12 months) carry slightly
    elevated migration risk as the borrower approaches final balloon payments.

10. interest_rate_pct
    Annual interest rate on the loan at the assessment date (for variable rate
    products, this is the current rate including any tracker margin).
    Range: 1.5 %–35.0 %. Credit cards average ~21.5 %; mortgages ~4.2 %.

11. loan_to_value_pct
    Current loan-to-value ratio: outstanding balance divided by the most
    recent assessed collateral value, expressed as a percentage.
    Applies to: Residential Mortgage, Buy-to-Let Mortgage, Auto Finance,
    SME Term Loan (where secured against assets).
    Range: 30 %–100 %. Value -1 = not applicable (unsecured product).

12. is_fixed_rate
    Whether the loan bears a fixed interest rate (as opposed to variable,
    tracker, or base-rate-linked).
    Values: Yes | No
    Fixed-rate borrowers are insulated from rate rises in the short term;
    variable-rate borrowers face immediate payment pressure in rising
    rate environments.

13. vintage_year
    The calendar year in which the loan was originated. Older vintages
    originated during lower credit standards or prior to a rate cycle may
    carry elevated migration risk.
    Range: 2004–2024.

── Borrower Credit Profile ───────────────────────────────────────────────────

14. internal_credit_score
    The bank's proprietary credit score for the borrower at the assessment
    date, incorporating payment history, utilisation, credit depth, and
    bureau data.
    Range: 300–900. Higher scores indicate stronger creditworthiness.
    Scores below 600 are associated with materially elevated migration risk.

15. credit_score_change_last_quarter
    Point change in the internal credit score over the preceding quarter
    (positive = improvement, negative = deterioration). A decline of more
    than 20 points is a meaningful early warning signal.
    Range: -120 to +80.

16. bureau_inquiries_last_6m
    Number of external credit bureau inquiries (hard searches) recorded
    against the borrower in the 6 months before the assessment date.
    High search frequency can indicate financial stress or active credit
    shopping.
    Range: 0–12.

17. has_other_credit_with_bank
    Whether the borrower holds one or more additional credit products with
    this bank (e.g., current account, credit card, second mortgage). Enables
    behavioural cross-product monitoring.
    Values: Yes | No

── Payment Behaviour ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

18. days_past_due_current
    Number of days the borrower's most recent payment is past due at the
    assessment date. All loans in Stage 1 are capped at 29 DPD (30+ DPD
    triggers automatic Stage 2 classification per IFRS 9 backstop).
    Range: 0–29 days. 88 % of loans are fully current (0 DPD).
    DPD buckets by migration rate:
      0 DPD → 15.9 %  |  1-6 DPD → 58.8 %  |  7-13 DPD → 71.8 %  |
      14-29 DPD → 80.2 %

19. missed_payments_last_12m
    Total number of missed or returned payment events in the 12 months before
    the assessment date.
    Range: 0–8. Loans with 2+ missed payments have a 57.7 % migration rate
    vs 10.3 % for loans with zero missed payments.

20. payment_reduction_requested
    Whether the borrower requested a formal payment reduction, holiday, or
    deferral in the 12 months before the assessment date.
    Values: Yes | No
    Strong distress signal: 54.3 % migration rate (Yes) vs 17.9 % (No).

21. forbearance_history
    Whether the borrower has been granted any formal forbearance arrangement
    (e.g., interest-only period, payment moratorium, term extension) at any
    point in the past 3 years.
    Values: Yes | No. Migration rate: 44.7 % (Yes) vs 19.0 % (No).

22. utilisation_rate_pct
    For revolving credit products (Credit Card, Corporate Revolving Credit
    Facility): current balance as a percentage of the credit limit.
    High utilisation (> 80 %) signals financial stress.
    Range: 1 %–100 %. Value -1 = not applicable (non-revolving product).

── Macro and External Triggers ──────────────────────────────────────────────

23. sector_stress_flag
    For SME and corporate borrowers: whether the borrower's primary operating
    sector has been flagged by the bank's credit risk team as under elevated
    macroeconomic or sector-specific stress (e.g., commercial real estate,
    hospitality, retail).
    Values: Yes | No

24. property_value_change_pct_last_qtr
    For mortgage products: percentage change in the property's assessed
    market value over the prior quarter, derived from the bank's automated
    valuation model. Negative values indicate falling collateral values.
    Range: -12 % to +10 %. Value -999 = not applicable (non-mortgage).

25. interest_rate_environment
    The prevailing interest rate environment at the assessment date, classified
    from the Bank of England base rate trajectory.
    Values: Rising | Stable | Falling
    Rising rate environments are associated with higher migration rates (26.8 %
    vs ~19 % in stable/falling environments), particularly for variable-rate
    borrowers facing payment shock.

── IFRS 9 Quantitative Triggers ─────────────────────────────────────────────

26. pd_12m_at_origination_pct
    The 12-month probability of default (PD) model output for this loan at
    the time of origination. Forms the baseline against which Significant
    Increase in Credit Risk (SICR) is measured under IFRS 9.
    Range: 0.05 %–15.0 %.

27. pd_12m_current_pct
    The 12-month PD model output recalculated at the assessment date,
    incorporating current payment behaviour, credit score, macroeconomic
    overlays, and sectoral factors.
    Range: 0.02 %–30.0 %. A key IFRS 9 staging input.

28. pd_relative_change_pct
    Percentage change in the 12-month PD relative to the origination PD:
    (pd_12m_current - pd_12m_at_origination) / pd_12m_at_origination × 100.
    The primary IFRS 9 SICR quantitative threshold — many banks use a doubling
    of PD (> 100 % relative change) as a mandatory Stage 2 trigger.
    Range: -90 % to +1,500 %.
    Migration by band: < 0% → 2.8% | 0–50% → 7.1% | 50–100% → 15.1% |
                       100–200% → 44.7% | > 200% → 79.1%

29. ltv_change_pct_since_origination
    For secured loans: change in the LTV ratio since origination (positive =
    LTV has increased, i.e., collateral has declined relative to balance).
    Rising LTV signals reduced collateral coverage and elevated loss-given-
    default.
    Range: -30 % to +40 %. Value -999 = not applicable.

── Loan History and Borrower Behaviour ──────────────────────────────────────

30. is_restructured_loan
    Whether the loan terms have been formally restructured at any point since
    origination (e.g., rate reduction, term extension, capitalisation of
    arrears). Restructured loans carry a higher migration rate (36.5 % vs
    20.9 % for non-restructured).
    Values: Yes | No

31. months_since_last_restructure
    For restructured loans: months elapsed since the most recent formal
    restructuring event. Recent restructures (< 6 months) carry higher
    residual risk. Set to 0 for non-restructured loans.
    Range: 0–36 months.

32. early_repayment_flag
    Whether the borrower has made one or more early or overpayments in the
    3 months before the assessment date, reducing the balance ahead of
    schedule. A protective signal indicating financial capacity and borrower
    engagement.
    Values: Yes | No. Migration rate: 10.0 % (Yes) vs 24.4 % (No).

33. current_account_debit_flag
    Whether the borrower's main current account (held with this bank) went
    into an unplanned debit balance or exceeded its overdraft limit in the
    prior quarter. A strong cross-product financial distress indicator.
    Values: Yes | No

34. origination_ltv_pct
    The LTV ratio at the time of loan origination. Higher origination LTVs
    indicate lower equity cushion from inception and greater vulnerability to
    collateral value declines.
    Range: 40 %–99 %. Value -1 = not applicable (unsecured product).

35. months_on_book
    Number of months the loan has been active since origination.
    Range: 3 to original_loan_term_months.

36. stage_2_migration
    Binary variable.
    1 = The loan was reclassified from Stage 1 (12-month ECL) to Stage 2
        (lifetime ECL) at the next quarterly IFRS 9 assessment. This is
        triggered either by a quantitative SICR test (pd_relative_change > 100 %),
        a qualitative SICR trigger (payment arrears, forbearance, restructuring),
        or the IFRS 9 backstop rule (30+ days past due). The reclassification
        requires the bank to recognise a significantly larger loss provision.
    0 = The loan remained in Stage 1 at the next assessment date.
    Overall migration rate : ~22.0 %
